The Basics of Japanese

To start it simply, Japanese is the native language of Japan. The language today has 3 scripts. Hiragana, Katakana, and Kanji. Hiragana and Katakana are "Kana", which are symbols which represent the syllables and sounds in the Japanese language. Kanji are the logographs taken from the Chinese language and adapted to Japanese to represent items, ideas, or concepts.

Kanji

Kanji appear mostly in things like nouns, adjectives, and verbs. Most of them have two different pronunciations; On-yomi/音読み and Kun-yomi/訓読み. On-yomi is the best attempt at Japanese people copying the Chinese pronunciation in kana, and Kun-yomi is the original Japanese word for the concept. It's a bit confusing at first, but it will get easier to understand with time.

Kanji are consisted of things called radicals (部首), which are like pieces, that are combined to form kanji. Some hint to the overall meaning, while some just aid in understanding the prounciation.

Examples:

木 (tree) + 木 + 木 = 森 (forest)
子 (child) + 女 (woman) = 好 (fond of, to like)

版, 坂, 阪, 飯, 板 are all pronounced はん, and all contain 反 on the left!

All together, they can make a sentence like,

僕はコーヒーを好きです (I like coffee) or このウェブサイトはかっこいいです (This website is cool)
