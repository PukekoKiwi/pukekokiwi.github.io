The Basics of Japanese

To start it simply, Japanese is the native language of Japan. The language today has 3 scripts. Hiragana, Katakana, and Kanji. Hiragana and Katakana are "Kana", which are symbols which represent the syllables and sounds in the Japanese language. Kanji are the logographs taken from the Chinese language and adapted to Japanese to represent items, ideas, or concepts.

Kanji

Kanji appear mostly in things like nouns, adjectives, and verbs. Most of them have two different pronunciations; On-yomi and Kun-yomi. On-yomi is the best attempt at Japanese people copying the Chinese pronunciation in kana.
